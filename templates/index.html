About Us

This research seeks to examine the effects of various psychological states, including stress, anxiety, and mood, on neurocognitive functions such as problem-solving and memory retention. Drawing on findings from prior studies, we posit that a positive and relaxed mental state may facilitate enhanced problem-solving capabilities and more effective memory retention. This study aims to expand the understanding of the interplay between emotional well-being and cognitive performance, with potential implications for psychological and educational interventions. This study is being led by Professor Ellen J. Langer, Department of Psychology, Harvard University.

What we will ask you to do

The study will be conducted in the following order. Participants will begin with a 10-minute survey to collect demographic information (e.g., gender, age, education) and complete a psychological assessment. This will be followed by a brief 5-minute “warm-up exercise” designed to prepare participants for the subsequent cognitive tasks. The main segment of the experiment will consist of a 20-minute session involving three distinct neuropsychological tasks aimed at evaluating various aspects of cognitive functioning. Finally, participants will complete a concluding survey to provide feedback and offer suggestions for potential future studies, alongside an additional psychological assessment.
